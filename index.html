Peace Corps/El Salvador

Water Project

The water project was the largest project during Peace Corps Service. The community built a new capture tank, installed two miles of new pipes, and built new separate systems for livestock. The PCV trained the community members on managing work groups, finances, inventory, and project management. Before the project was finished, community members would have water about twice a week. After the project was finished, household pipes were breaking because there was so much water.

Youth Development

The PCV engaged in youth development, organizing classes, workshops, and team-building activities for about 100 youth of all ages. They were challenged to think outside of the box culturally, present new ideas to their peers, and work together. The PCV was able to partner with Plan International and Aid El Salvador, providing educational opportunities that would not otherwise have been present in the community.
